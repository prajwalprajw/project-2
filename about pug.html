my story

Due to this, she has not yet been sterilized as it is not advisable to put such dogs under anaesthesia. However if you are a first-time pet parent then please read on….

Ollie had gone to a foster home and has also been adopted before. Both times, she was brought back to the shelter because she didn’t eat food nor did she want to pee/poop for a few days. She tends to get nervous in a home environment and needs you to be patient and committed and follow the advice given by the shelter to give her a chance to adjust in your home. So if you plan on expressing interest for Ollie, please note, that you will need to be put in some work before Ollie feels at home.
